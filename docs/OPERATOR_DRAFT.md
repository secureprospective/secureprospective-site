# Christopher Campbell

*Business must adapt or suffer the inevitable. I built SecureProspective to make the adaptation deliberate.*

## Three careers, one spine

**Technology is the passion.** It taught me to look forward — to see the future-state before it arrives and build toward it. This is the Transform movement of the method: not "AI-powered," but AI-native. Prefix dropped.

**Construction was the objective truth.** Fifteen years of residential electrical work. Voltage is or isn't. Code is right or wrong. There is no spin in electrical work — only what's safe and what kills. This taught me Diagnose: find what's actually broken before touching anything. Most consulting de-emphasizes this step. I emphasize it.

**Insurance was the responsibility.** Life, annuity, running an IMO. Stewardship for clients' long-term outcomes. This taught me Position and Shape: set up systems that last, and structure every output so the person who owns it can actually own it — not abdicate to a black box.

The four-movement method wasn't invented in a consulting deck. It was distilled from three careers that each taught one part of it.

## Proof

[TFM](/the-work#tfm) — Tech Freedom Ministries — is the first client of the consulting practice. A faith-backed digital freedom nonprofit. I built the digital infrastructure, brand system, and live site. It's the first place the method got applied to someone else's business.

The second proof is internal: [the IMO, distilled](/the-work#imo-distilled). I ran one. I knew what was broken. I applied the method to my own business first.
